4 Conversion Tactics to Kickstart Your Sales for the Holidays (& Beyond!)

The big story for the 2017 holiday season is in mobile shopping. Everyone is trying to find the best way to improve website engagement, conversion or sales. The wave of online shoppers this season was immense, $5.03 Billion was spent online on Black Friday and a whopping $6.59 Billion on Cyber Monday.

Conversion rates across all devices saw double digit growth throughout Black Friday. Notable in the mobile story is that a record-breaking $2 billion in sales were made via smartphones which was a 40% increase over last year, indicating that the gap between mobile traffic and revenue is closing.

Don't miss out on the biggest spending event of the year!

Traffic Alone Is Not Enough

If you’ve created great ads that drive traffic to your site for the holidays, kudos! But it’s only half of a the equation. As soon as your customers hit your homepage, you now have the opportunity to start building excitement about your brand and moving them towards your products or services. Bring some holiday magic to your website to encourage your visitors to get into the festive spirit!

Getting Conversion Ready For The Holidays

Prepare and Plan. It’s a good idea to know which products/services you will be discounting, how much the offer will be, when the sale will start, and when it will end. To optimize results, try upselling items at opportune times and bundling top sellers into packages.

Enhance the customer experience by using strategically timed and placed overlay campaigns on your website. These eye-catching experiences will engage your shoppers with personalized designs at opportune times!

Tactic #1: The Flash Sale (Instant Promotions)

This is the simplest and most widely used of all campaigns. The idea is to have a message launch almost instantly when a visitor lands on your site, making it virtually impossible to miss. This way you can direct traffic to your most compelling offer immediately by invoking the fear of missing out (FOMO) .

Key elements for running a flash sale on your site include:

1) Basic Targetting. Target everyone! Old and new visitors should see the message at least once a day.

2) Clear Offer. The offer should be the focal point of your message. If there are restrictions try including them subtly in the design without overwhelming the offer.

3) Simple Design. Contrasting colours and simplicity of design will make it easy for a visitor to digest quickly and take action. Avoid cluttering the design with unnecessary images or text. Remember KISS (keep it simple stupid)!

4) Strong Call to Action. There should only be one. Recall Hicks Law, providing too many options can deter the user experience as it increases the time it takes to make a deicision. There is only CTA in the example, providing a shortcut so visitors can easily take advantage of the offer.

5) Start and End Date. This could be a date or a time range, or both! In this example it’s from 8 AM to 10 PM, weekend only.

Tactic #2: Giveaway Campaigns

People love free shit. It's no wonder then that giveaway campaigns are the highest converting campaign type, period.

A useful hack to holding a giveaway contest without impacting your bottom line is to not give away your best selling items because otherwise, it's less likely they'll purchase the item from your website. The best giveaways bundle together accessories that jazz up the product - a nice to have, instead of the core offering.

Want to sky-rocket your email collection? Just follow in the footsteps of GNC.ca who collected 600+ emails in a week (17% conversion rate) or Oneiric Hockey who have seen a 300% boost in email capture after installing Aiva!

Spice up thse flash sales and giveaways with countdowns! Encourage procrastinating visitors to make a decision now using a ticking clock to coax them to act.

Tactic #3: The Free Shipping Offer

If you don’t already offer free shipping, consider it. According to Kissmetrics, free shipping can raise a company's net profit by 30% if done correctly.

“66% of online shoppers have decided NOT to buy an item because of shipping costs.” (Source: BigCommerce)

If you’re still not sure, then add a minimum order value. In general, customers are willing to spend more money rather than pay an additional amount for shipping.

Try supercharging this tactic by combining it with a sense of scarcity and only offer free shipping during the holiday to make sure visitors don't miss out a unique opportunity.

Tactic #4: Cart Abandonment Campaigns

As an online retailer there is nothing more frustrating than knowing a visitor simply decided to walk away at the last moment.

By targeting your checkout pages and triggering based on user behaviour, you can design a captivating messae with the goal of saving the sale. A campaign could appear when the visitor show intent of leaving and read something like, “Don’t go! Complete your order now and save 10% off your purchase”. Don't like giving out discounts? Try prompting visitors to purchase with a testimonial that slides in after 10 seconds of inactivity instead.

This speaks directly to what the visitor is experiencing and adds an extra incentive to purchase now, to ensure you're taking your best shot to make a sale, every time.

Gift-Wrapping It Up

Considering that the holiday season accounts for more sales than Mother’s Day, Father’s Day, Valentine’s Day, Halloween, Easter and St. Patrick’s Day combined, retailers should strive to provide the best experience possible to their customers during this time of year. With a boost in web traffic during the holidays, 'tis the season to optimize your conversion!

A happy customer who enjoys a pleasant interaction with your website today will, undoubtedly, return in the future. So even if you have short-term goals in mind, think about the potential long-term gains as well.
